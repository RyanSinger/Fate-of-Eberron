## Boromar Clan

Sharn's dominant criminal syndicate. Halfling run, deeply embedded in Lower Dura's economy. They control gambling, smuggling, and protection rackets. Currently losing territory to Daask.

### Saidan Boromar (he/him)

**Role:** Leader | **Tier:** Boss

The aging patriarch. Sharp mind, failing body. He built the Boromar empire from a smuggling operation into Sharn's shadow government. Every politician in Lower Dura owes him. Every merchant pays tribute. Now his health is failing and everyone smells blood. He holds the clan together through loyalty and fear, but loyalty fades and fear needs reinforcement.

**Hook:** The PCs need something only the Boromar can provide, or they've accidentally crossed into Boromar business. Either way, Saidan wants a conversation.

**Aspects:**
- *Patriarch of Sharn's Shadow Empire*
- *My Body Is Failing but My Mind Is Not*
- *Loyalty Bought with Gold and Decades of Favors*
- *Daask Will Not Take What I Built*
- *Everyone Wants What Comes After Me*

**Skills:**
- Contacts Fantastic (+6)
- Resources Superb (+5)
- Will Great (+4), Deceive Great (+4)
- Rapport Good (+3), Notice Good (+3)
- Lore Fair (+2), Empathy Fair (+2)

**Stunts:**
- **I Know People Who Know People**: +2 to Contacts when calling in a favor from Sharn's criminal underworld.
- **Old Man's Gambit**: Once per scene, use Contacts instead of Deceive to create an advantage by revealing a hidden connection or obligation.
- **Shadow Government**: +2 to Resources when operating in Lower Dura.

### Hallia Boromar (she/her)

**Role:** Rival | **Tier:** Hitter

Saidan's niece and likely successor. Ambitious, modern, willing to make deals her uncle wouldn't. She's already positioning herself for the transition, building alliances with factions Saidan would never trust. She smiles too easily and means it too rarely. The clan elders underestimate her because she's young. That's a mistake they'll make once.

**Hook:** Hallia approaches the PCs with an offer that's too good. She needs outside help for something she can't let the clan know about.

*Boromar Heir with Modern Ambitions*
*Underestimated by Everyone Who Should Know Better*

**Skills:**
- Deceive Great (+4)
- Rapport Good (+3), Contacts Good (+3)
- Will Fair (+2), Notice Fair (+2)

**Stunt:** **Better Offer**: +2 to Rapport when proposing a deal that undercuts a rival's arrangement.

### Castar (he/him)

**Role:** Enforcer | **Tier:** Hitter

Boromar's chief leg-breaker. A halfling who doesn't fit the stereotype: tall for his kind, quiet, and terrifyingly efficient. He handles problems Saidan wants handled without noise. Never threatens. Never postures. Just arrives, does the work, and leaves. The people who've seen him work don't talk about it. The people who talk about it haven't seen him work.

**Hook:** Castar shows up to deliver a message. The message is a warning. The next visit won't involve conversation.

*The Quiet Hand of the Boromar*
*Efficiency Over Spectacle*

**Skills:**
- Fight Great (+4)
- Stealth Good (+3), Athletics Good (+3)
- Notice Fair (+2)

**Stunt:** **No Witnesses**: +2 to Stealth when leaving the scene of a completed job.

### Miri Quickfingers (she/her)

**Role:** Informant | **Tier:** Threat

*Street Rat Who Knows Too Much*
Skills: Notice Average (+1), Stealth Average (+1)
A street urchin who runs messages for the Boromar. Knows every alley in Callestan. Will sell information to anyone, including you, including your enemies. Twelve years old and already smarter about Sharn's streets than most adults.

**Hook:** Miri has information about a case, but her price isn't gold. She needs a favor.

## Daask

A monstrous crime syndicate backed by the Daughters of Sora Kell in Droaam. Ogres, gnolls, medusas, and changelings pushing into Boromar territory. Brutal but not stupid. They want legitimacy as much as they want territory, and they'll break whatever stands between them and a seat at the table.

### Cavallah (she/her)

**Role:** Leader | **Tier:** Boss

An ogre mage who runs Daask operations in Sharn. Genuinely believes Droaam deserves a seat at the table. Uses violence strategically, not gratuitously. Smarter than anyone expects, which is how she likes it. She plays the brute when it serves her and the diplomat when it doesn't. Most people never realize they're dealing with the same person.

**Hook:** Cavallah wants to make a deal. She's offering something the PCs need. The price is helping Daask with a problem, which means working against the Boromar.

**Aspects:**
- *Ogre Mage Running Sharn's Monster Syndicate*
- *Smarter Than You Think, and I Like It That Way*
- *Droaam Deserves a Seat at the Table*
- *Violence Is a Tool, Not a Lifestyle*
- *The Daughters of Sora Kell Are Watching*

**Skills:**
- Provoke Fantastic (+6)
- Will Superb (+5)
- Fight Great (+4), Deceive Great (+4)
- Physique Good (+3), Notice Good (+3)
- Contacts Fair (+2), Lore Fair (+2)

**Stunts:**
- **Ogre Mage Sorcery**: Once per scene, use Will to create a magical advantage (invisibility, darkness, cone of cold) with a free invoke.
- **Calculated Brutality**: +2 to Provoke when making an example of someone to send a message.
- **Monstrous Resilience**: Armor:1 against physical attacks.

Cavallah fights when she chooses to, and she chooses well. She prefers to negotiate, but negotiation backed by the credible threat of an ogre mage is its own kind of violence.

### Ash (they/them)

**Role:** Enforcer | **Tier:** Hitter

A changeling assassin. Daask's solution for problems that can't be solved with ogre strength. Nobody knows their real face. They may have already been someone you trusted. Ash doesn't enjoy killing, but they're very good at it, and Cavallah pays well enough that enjoyment is beside the point.

**Hook:** Someone the PCs dealt with recently turns out to have been Ash. What did they learn?

*The Face You Never See Twice*
*Nobody Knows the Real Me (Including Me)*

**Skills:**
- Deceive Great (+4)
- Stealth Good (+3), Fight Good (+3)
- Notice Fair (+2), Athletics Fair (+2)

**Stunt:** **Thousand Faces**: +2 to Deceive when impersonating a specific person the target has met before.

### Grakk's Pack (mixed)

**Role:** Muscle | **Tier:** Filler (6 members)

*Gnoll Pack Holding Malleon's Gate*
Skills: Fight Fair (+2), Physique Fair (+2), Notice Average (+1)
Stress: [ ] [ ] [ ] [ ] [ ] [ ]
A gnoll street gang, about six strong, that controls two blocks in Malleon's Gate. Loyal to Cavallah. Surprisingly disciplined when they need to be. They know every rooftop, every alley, every escape route in their territory. Outsiders don't pass through without Grakk's say.

**Hook:** The PCs need to cross Daask territory. Grakk's Pack is the checkpoint.

### The Butcher of Fallen (he/him)

**Role:** Wild Card | **Tier:** Hitter

A minotaur pit fighter who handles Daask's most public violence. Theatrical. Enjoys his reputation. Secretly exhausted by it. He plays the monster because that's what Cavallah needs and what the crowds want. Late at night he reads poetry in a language nobody in Sharn speaks.

**Hook:** The Butcher is fighting in tonight's pit match. Someone wants him to lose. Someone else wants him to win. Both are paying.

*The Monster They Paid to See*
*Tired of the Blood but Not the Applause*

**Skills:**
- Fight Great (+4)
- Physique Good (+3), Provoke Good (+3)
- Athletics Fair (+2)

**Stunt:** **Crowd Favorite**: +2 to Provoke when fighting in front of an audience.
