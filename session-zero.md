An example Session Zero. The same five players and characters from the [Play Example](play-example) sit down to build their charter, sort out the kind of game they want, and make their characters together. Read this first to see where those characters came from.

---

## The Table

Five players and a GM. Nobody has made a character yet.

- **Mara**, who will play Vex
- **Devin**, who will play Kira
- **Sam**, who will play Theron
- **Priya**, who will play Kaela
- **Tom**, who will play Jak

They haven't decided any of that yet. That's what Session Zero is for.

---

## Part 1: The Pitch

**GM**: Here's what I'm thinking. Two years after the Last War, in Sharn. You run a small inquisitive agency out of Lower Dura, the part of the city the Watch doesn't bother with. People come to you with the cases nobody else will touch: missing persons the Watch wrote off, debts the courts won't enforce, secrets that could get someone killed. Noir. Mystery. Favors and debts. You'll cross dragonmarked houses, crime syndicates, and the occasional thing left over from the war that should have stayed buried.

**Priya**: So we're the people you call when you can't call the law.

**GM**: Exactly. You're not heroes. You're the ones who show up.

**Devin**: I love it. Casablanca with airships.

**GM**: That's the energy. Before anyone makes a character, let's agree on the kind of game this is, then build the agency together. Characters come last, once we all know what we're sitting inside of.

---

## Part 2: Safety Tools

**GM**: Eberron gets dark. War crimes, conspiracies, the things in Khyber. Before we go anywhere, let's set boundaries. I'll put an X-Card on the table. Anyone can tap it, any time, no explanation. We rewind or skip whatever crossed the line.

**Sam**: Works for me.

**GM**: Lines and veils. Lines are things we keep out entirely. Veils are things that can happen, but off-screen. Anything anyone wants as a line?

**Mara**: Harm to children stays off-screen. Veil, not a line, but I don't want it described.

**Priya**: Agreed. And torture as a veil too.

**GM**: Done. Both veiled. Anyone want a hard line?

**Tom**: Nothing from me as a line. The war stuff is the reason I'm here, I just want it to mean something, not be set dressing.

**GM**: Noted, and that actually tells me how to run it. Let's write that down: the war has weight. Good. X-Card stays out all session, every session.

---

## Part 3: Story Elements

**GM**: Now the worksheet. Three steps. First, inspirations. Throw out anything that excites you. No bad answers.

**Devin**: Leverage. The competent-crew, con-of-the-week thing.

**Sam**: The Third Man. Postwar city, everybody compromised.

**Priya**: Redemption arcs. People who did bad things in the war trying to be worth something now.

**Mara**: Blade Runner. What counts as a person.

**Tom**: Found family. Five people who shouldn't trust anyone, trusting each other.

**GM**: Great spread. Writing all of it down. Now the ingredients. I'll read Eberron's big themes and we sort each one: keeper, deal breaker, or conditional.

The group works down the list.

| Ingredient | Verdict |
|------------|---------|
| **Tone: noir mystery** | Keeper |
| **Tone: pulp action** | Keeper (mixed with the noir) |
| **Scope: city (Sharn)** | Keeper. Stays local. No continental plots yet |
| **The Last War** | Keeper, as an active wound. Veteran trauma is on the table, handled with care |
| **Warforged personhood** | Keeper. Central, given Vex |
| **Dragonmarked houses** | Keeper. House Cannith especially |
| **Crime syndicates** | Keeper. The Boromar Clan runs Lower Dura |
| **The Mourning** | Keeper as background and personal stakes, not something we solve |
| **Horror: body horror** | Conditional. Cosmic and uncanny is fine; gore is veiled |
| **The Draconic Prophecy** | Conditional. Background mystery only, not a plot engine |
| **Planar travel** | Deal breaker for now. We stay in Sharn |

**GM**: Last step. We distill all that into a few campaign aspects. These are true every session, always invokable, always compellable. Two to four.

The table lands on four:

- *The Treaty of Thronehold Is Held Together by Lies*
- *Sharn Runs on Favors and Debts*
- *The Mourning Could Happen Again*
- *Nobody Trusts What the War Made*

**Mara**: That last one is going to bite Vex constantly.

**GM**: That's the idea. It's a fate point any time it does.

---

## Part 4: Build the Charter

**GM**: Now the agency. We build this before characters so everyone makes someone who already belongs here. We need a framework, territory, a headquarters, a community, a patron, an antagonist, and a reputation.

**Framework.** Agency, obviously. An inquisitive shop.

**Territory.** Middle and Lower Dura, Sharn's poorest quarter.

**Devin**: Give it an issue aspect. *The Watch Doesn't Come Down Here.*

**GM**: That's the whole pitch in one line. Keeping it.

**Headquarters.** 

**Tom**: Somewhere cheap. A cramped office above a shop.

**Priya**: Above a noodle shop in Callestan. It smells like broth all day.

**GM**: HQ aspect: *Everyone Knows Where to Find Us.* Double-edged. Clients find you easily. So do people who want you dead.

**Community.** The working people of Dura, the ones who can't afford the Watch's attention and wouldn't get it anyway. That's who you protect, and who brings you work.

**Patron.** You need someone who feeds you cases.

**Sam**: A message clerk. Someone who hears everything.

**GM**: Sevren, a House Sivis clerk at the local message station. He routes the jobs that come in through the speaking stones and don't have anywhere legitimate to go. Fair (+2) Contacts, knows everyone's business, owes you for something we'll figure out in play.

**Antagonist.** Someone you've already tangled with. Not the campaign's final boss, just a recurring problem.

**Devin**: The Boromar Clan runs the ward. Give us a mid-level boss who hates us.

**GM**: A Boromar underboss who works Callestan. You've cost him money before. He hasn't forgotten. We'll name him when he shows up.

**Reputation.** What do people say about you?

**Priya**: *The Ones Who Take the Cases Nobody Else Will.*

**GM**: That's it. That's the agency.

> **The Dura Eye**
> - **Framework**: Agency
> - **Territory**: Middle and Lower Dura. *The Watch Doesn't Come Down Here*
> - **HQ**: A cramped office above a noodle shop in Callestan. *Everyone Knows Where to Find Us*
> - **Community**: The working people of Dura who can't afford the Watch's attention
> - **Patron**: Sevren, a House Sivis message clerk who routes the cases nobody else will take
> - **Antagonist**: A Boromar underboss in Callestan the party has crossed before
> - **Reputation**: *The Ones Who Take the Cases Nobody Else Will*

Each player will pick one Charter stunt from the Agency list when they finish their character.

---

## Part 5: Sketch the Region

**GM**: Quick map before we make characters. Not a hex grid, a relationship map. The office is home base, it's in your network automatically. Then a few periphery sites: places you know but haven't locked down. Each one is a hook.

- **The Dura Eye office** (home base, Callestan). Power: Average (+1). Skills: Contacts, Investigate. *Everyone Knows Where to Find Us.*
- **The Broken Anvil** (Middle Dura). A tavern and unofficial message board for working folk. Where half your cases walk in the door. *Watchful Eyes, Loose Tongues.*
- **The Gilded Cage** (Callestan). A Boromar gambling den, the underboss's turf. Hostile. *Everyone Here Owes Somebody.*
- **Sevren's message station** (Lower Dura). House Sivis. Your pipeline for work, if you keep Sevren happy.

**GM**: The Broken Anvil is going to matter sooner than you think. Hold onto it.

---

## Part 6: Make Your Characters

**GM**: Now we go around. Pick an archetype, that's what you do now. Pick a background, that's where you come from, and it should add color the archetype doesn't, not repeat it. Add a species if you're not human. Write your five aspects. As people share, look for connections. That's where your relationship aspects come from.

### Mara builds Vex

**Mara**: I want the Blade Runner angle. A warforged. Built to fight a war that's over, and now what? Archetype: Inquisitive. He's a detective. He notices everything and can't stop.

**GM**: Background?

**Mara**: War Veteran. Not Warrior, I don't want him to be a fighter, that's not the point of him. The war is what he's *from*. It's in him, the watchfulness, the discipline, the not-sleeping. The detective work is what he chose after.

**GM**: That's exactly the split. War Veteran gives you the marks of the war without making you a second Warrior. High Concept?

**Mara**: *Warforged Detective Who Sees Too Much.* Motivation: *I Have to Know What Peace Makes Me.* Background aspect: *Forged for the Last War, Adrift in the Peace.* And a free aspect, *Cold Logic, Warm Justice.*

**Devin**: Wait. How did a warforged end up running a detective agency in Lower Dura? Somebody had to keep him out of the scrap yards. A lot of warforged got decommissioned after the Treaty.

**Mara**: ...yeah. Somebody pulled me out of the Cogs before I got melted down.

**Devin**: That was Kira. I'll take it from the other side.

**Mara**: Relationship: *Kira Saved Me from the Cogs.* That's five aspects.

**GM**: Stunts. One archetype, one background, one charter.

**Mara**: Inquisitive: *Photographic Memory*, +2 Investigate recalling details from an earlier scene. War Veteran: *Tireless Sentinel*, I never sleep, +2 Notice spotting ambushes. Charter: *We Know This Town.* Notice goes at the top of my pyramid.

### Devin builds Kira

**Devin**: Kira's the one who saves people quietly. Archetype: Spy. Cyran intelligence, except there's no Cyre to report to anymore. The Mourning took her whole country.

**GM**: So Spy is the calling. Background?

**Devin**: Refugee. That's the *where she's from* now: she lost everything, same as a million other Cyrans. It's not a spy background, it's the hole the war left.

**GM**: Good, those don't overlap. The Spy is what she does, the Refugee is the wound under it. Aspects?

**Devin**: High Concept: *Cyran Intelligence Agent Without a Country.* Motivation: *The Mourning Took My Family; I'll Find Out Why.* Background: *Trained by the King's Dark Lanterns.* Free aspect: *I Have Contacts Everywhere.* And the relationship with Vex: *Vex Is the Only One I Trust Completely.*

**Tom**: Can I hook in here? Jak needed to get out of Sharn fast once. If Kira has contacts everywhere and trusts almost nobody...

**Devin**: I got you out. That tracks.

**GM**: So Kira is linked to two of you, Vex and Jak. That's fine. With five players, one character carries an extra bond. Kira's the hub.

**Devin**: Stunts. Spy: *Dead Drop*, once a session I declare a contact in the city who owes me. Refugee: *Unreadable*, I learned to bury what I feel to survive, +2 Deceive resisting attempts to read me. Charter: *The Office Never Closes.* Will at the top.

### Tom builds Jak

**Tom**: Jak's the war-stuff-means-something piece for me. Archetype: Artificer. He's House Cannith. *Was* House Cannith. He got excoriated, cast out, and now the house wants him back or dead because of what he knows how to build.

**GM**: Background?

**Tom**: Criminal. On the run, you do what you have to. It's not a second calling, it's the life he fell into after the house threw him out.

**GM**: And the mark?

**Tom**: Aberrant Mark of Making. Not a clean dragonmark, a twisted one. That's a free aspect: *Mark of Making (Aberrant Variant).* It lets me jury-rig and repair things on instinct. High Concept: *Excoriated Cannith Artificer on the Run.* Motivation: *I'll Prove What I Am Without the House.* Background: *House Cannith Wants Me Back, or Dead.* Relationship with Kira: *Kira Got Me Out of Sharn When the House Came for Me.*

**GM**: Stunts.

**Tom**: Artificer: *Maker's Intuition*, +2 Investigate examining built things. Criminal: *Case the Joint*, +2 Burglary sizing up a place before I go in. Charter: *The Office Never Closes.* Crafts at the top.

### Priya builds Kaela

**Priya**: I want the redemption angle, from the soldier's side. Kaela was a Cyran noble's daughter, an officer in the war. The Mourning erased everything she was fighting for. Now she's a sword for hire. Archetype: Warrior. Background: Noble, that's where she's from, the title nobody honors anymore.

**GM**: Warrior plus Noble. The fighting is the calling, the bloodline is the color. Good. Aspects?

**Priya**: High Concept: *Cyran Noble Turned Mercenary.* Motivation: *House Deneith Owes Me Answers, and I'll Collect.* Background: *The Mourning Took Everything but My Sword.* Free: *Karrnathi Swordsmanship Training.*

**Sam**: Theron and Kaela should know each other. I was a Silver Flame priest in the war. What if we were both at Cyre when it happened?

**Priya**: You pulled me out of something. Or I pulled you out. Both?

**GM**: It can be both. The rules want you each to read the bond your own way. So pick your angle.

**Priya**: Kaela's proud. She'd never admit someone saved her. But she pays her debts. Relationship: *I Owe Theron a Life Debt from Cyre.*

**Sam**: And Theron remembers it the other way around: she saved *him*, and it's the reason he still has any faith left.

**GM**: Perfect. Same day at Cyre, two people each certain they owe the other. That's a relationship that'll never stop generating drama. Priya, stunts?

**Priya**: Warrior: *Cyran Blade Dance*, +2 Fight when outnumbered. Noble: *Noble Bearing*, +2 Rapport on first impressions. Charter: *We Know This Town.* Fight at the top.

### Sam builds Theron

**Sam**: Theron's the heaviest one. He's a priest of the Silver Flame, and he marched in the Purge, the campaign against supposed heretics. He did things in the name of the Flame he can't take back. Archetype: Priest. Background: War Veteran, because the Purge is the war for him, that's where his guilt lives.

**GM**: Priest as the calling, War Veteran as the scar. Aspects?

**Sam**: High Concept: *Silver Flame Priest Seeking Redemption.* Motivation: *Never Again Will I Let Zeal Become Slaughter.* Background: *I Marched in the Purge.* Free: *The Flame Burns Within.* Relationship with Kaela: *Kaela Saved My Life; I Owe Her My Faith.*

**GM**: Stunts.

**Sam**: Priest: *Shield of Faith*, I use Will instead of Athletics to defend against physical attacks, my belief is armor. War Veteran: *Sense Evil*, I hunted fiends and undead during the Purge, +2 Notice detecting supernatural corruption. Charter: *Street Cred.* Will at the top.

## What Session Zero Produced

By the end of the night, before a single die was rolled, the table had:

- **A shared game**: noir mystery in postwar Sharn, the war as an active wound, four campaign aspects everyone agreed to.
- **A charter**: The Dura Eye, an inquisitive agency in Lower Dura, with a home, a patron, an enemy, and a reputation.
- **A region**: a home base and three periphery hooks, one of them about to pay off.
- **Five characters who already belong together**: a warforged detective and the spy who saved him, the artificer she also smuggled out of the city, and two veterans of Cyre each convinced they owe the other their life.

Nobody made a character in a vacuum. Every relationship aspect points at someone else at the table. That's the point of building the charter first.

## The Hand-Off

**GM**: One more thing before we wrap. Your patron Sevren sent word an hour ago. A nervous gnome named Tolas wants to meet at the Broken Anvil. His business partner, a dwarf artificer, turned up dead. The Watch called it an accident. Tolas doesn't believe them.

**Mara**: A dead artificer. Tom's going to love this.

**Tom**: Oh no.

**GM**: That's where we'll start next time.
